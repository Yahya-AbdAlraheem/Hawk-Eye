1. Introduction

At Hawk Eye, we believe in the power of community-driven security. We take the security of our systems and user data very seriously. Despite our best efforts to ensure the security of our systems, vulnerabilities may still exist.

We appreciate the security research community's help in identifying potential vulnerabilities and disclosing them to us responsibly. This policy is designed to provide guidance to security researchers and to establish a clear framework for responsible vulnerability disclosure.

We value collaboration with security researchers and are committed to working together to maintain the highest security standards.

2. Scope of Policy

This responsible disclosure policy applies to the following systems and services:

  • Hawk Eye website and web applications
  • Hawk Eye OSINT tools and APIs
  • User account interfaces and management systems
  • Mobile applications developed by Hawk Eye
  • Third-party services that we maintain

Some systems and services may be explicitly out of scope for security testing, including:

  • Systems or services hosted by third-party providers
  • Physical security controls and facilities
  • Social engineering attacks against our employees
  • Denial of service (DoS) attacks

If you are unsure whether a system or service is in scope, please contact us before proceeding with any security testing.

3. Security Testing Guidelines

When conducting security testing on our systems, we require all researchers to adhere to the following guidelines:

Do

  • Only test systems that are within the defined scope
  • Provide detailed reports with reproducible steps
  • Make a good faith effort to avoid privacy violations and data destruction
  • Use only your own accounts for testing
  • Wait for our confirmation before sharing any details publicly

Don't

  • Perform testing that may harm the reliability or integrity of our services
  • Access, modify, or destroy data that doesn't belong to you
  • Conduct social engineering or phishing attacks
  • Execute denial of service attacks
  • Use automated vulnerability scanners without prior approval

We expect all security researchers to abide by these guidelines. Testing activities that intentionally violate these guidelines may be considered unauthorized and potentially subject to legal action.

Responsible security research requires a careful balance between thorough testing and respecting system boundaries.

4. How to Report Vulnerabilities

If you believe you've found a security vulnerability in our systems, we encourage you to notify us as soon as possible. We greatly appreciate the time and effort of security researchers in helping us maintain the security of our services.

Reporting Process:

  1. Email submission: Send your findings to yhyybdalrhym13@gmail.com with the subject line "Security Vulnerability Report"
  2. Include detailed information: Provide clear steps to reproduce the vulnerability, affected components, and potential impact
  3. Evidence: Include screenshots, videos, or proof-of-concept code when applicable
  4. Contact information: Include your name, email, and any other relevant contact details for follow-up
  5. Stay available: Be available for questions and follow-up information as we investigate the issue

Please provide as much information as possible about the vulnerability to help us understand and reproduce the issue. 

Example vulnerability report format:

Subject: [Security Vulnerability Report] - [Brief Description]

- Vulnerability type: [e.g., XSS, CSRF, SQLi]
- URL/endpoint: [Where the vulnerability exists]
- Steps to reproduce: [Detailed explanation]
- Impact: [What could an attacker achieve?]
- Suggested mitigation: [Optional]
- Your name: [For recognition purposes]

5. What to Expect After Reporting

After you submit a vulnerability report, here's what you can expect from us:

  • Acknowledgment: We will acknowledge receipt of your report within 48 hours.
  • Evaluation: Our security team will evaluate the report to verify the vulnerability and determine its impact.
  • Updates: We will provide regular updates on our progress in addressing the vulnerability, typically every 5-7 business days.
  • Resolution timeline: We aim to resolve critical vulnerabilities within 30 days of verification, though complex issues may require more time.
  • Disclosure coordination: We will work with you to determine an appropriate disclosure timeline that allows us to address the vulnerability before any public disclosure.

We are committed to transparency in our vulnerability management process while ensuring the security of our users and systems.

6. Recognition and Rewards

We believe in recognizing the valuable contributions of security researchers who help us improve our security posture.

While we do not currently operate a formal bug bounty program with monetary rewards, we offer the following forms of recognition for responsible disclosure:

  • Hall of Fame: Inclusion in our security researcher hall of fame on our website (with your permission)
  • Acknowledgment: Public acknowledgment in security advisories related to the fixed vulnerability
  • References: Letters of reference or acknowledgment for professional portfolios upon request
  • Swag: Hawk Eye branded merchandise for significant findings

The level of recognition provided will be determined based on the severity of the vulnerability and the quality of the report.

We value the contributions of ethical security researchers and are committed to providing appropriate recognition for their efforts.

7. Legal Safe Harbor

To encourage responsible disclosure, we provide the following safe harbor provisions for security researchers who act in accordance with this policy:

  • We will not pursue legal action against researchers who make a good faith effort to comply with this policy.
  • We will not pursue civil action or initiate a complaint to law enforcement for accidental, good faith violations of this policy.
  • We consider security research conducted under this policy to be authorized conduct under applicable computer crime laws.
  • If legal action is initiated by a third party against a researcher for activities conducted under this policy, we will make a good faith effort to make it known that the researcher's actions were conducted in compliance with this policy.

Please note that this safe harbor applies only to legal claims under our control and does not bind third parties.

8. Exclusions and Limitations

While we appreciate security research efforts, certain types of testing and vulnerabilities fall outside the scope of this policy:

  • Denial of Service testing: DoS testing is explicitly prohibited due to potential service disruption.
  • Physical testing: Physical security testing of our offices or data centers is not authorized.
  • Social engineering: Testing that involves social engineering of our employees, contractors, or vendors is prohibited.
  • Third-party services: Testing against third-party services we use but do not control is not authorized.
  • Automated scanning: Mass vulnerability scanners without prior approval are not permitted.

Additionally, the following vulnerability types are generally considered out of scope unless they pose a significant risk:

  • Descriptive error messages or server information disclosure
  • HTTP 404 codes or other standard HTTP error codes
  • Banner information identifying server or technology stack
  • Self-XSS that requires user interaction
  • Missing security headers that don't lead to an exploitable vulnerability

9. Policy Updates

All modifications to this policy are effective immediately upon posting. It is your responsibility to review this policy periodically to ensure you are aware of the current terms.
